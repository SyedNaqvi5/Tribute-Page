A quick overview of Khan's life so far

  • 1952 - Imran Ahmed Khan Niazi (a.k.a Imran Khan) was born in Lahore on 5 October.
  • 1972 - He enrolled in Keble College, Oxford where he studied Philosophy, Politics and Economics, graduating two years later.
  • 1973 - Khan made his cricket debut at the age of 16 in Lahore. He played for his home teams. He was also part of the University of Oxford's Blues Cricket team.
  • 1982 - At the height of his career the thirty-year-old Khan took over the captaincy of the Pakistan cricket team.
  • 1991 - He single-handedly campaigned and collected money for his cancer hospital Shaukat Khanam. Since then the hospital has thrived and opened more branches of itself. Along with this he started other projects making him one of the biggest philanthropist in the world.
  • 1992 - His career-high as a captain and cricketer came when he led Pakistan to victory Cricket World Cup. Playing with a brittle batting line-up, Khan took the winning last wicket himself.
  • 1995 - Khan married Jemima Goldsmith, in a two-minute ceremony conducted in Urdu in Paris. A month later, they were married again in a civil ceremony at the Richmond registry office in England. The couple have two sons, Sulaiman Isa and Kasim. However, they ended their 9 year long marriage as Jemima was facing severe allegations from Khan's opponents.
  • 1996 - Imran Khan founded a political party, Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) due to rising corruption, injustices and mafias in the country. He won from the NA seat from Mianwali several years later.
